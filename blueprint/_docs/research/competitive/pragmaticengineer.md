## What they do well

- **Newsletter-first hierarchy, blog-second.** Home page is a conversion landing (Subscribe button center-screen, "1 million readers" social proof, "Hi, I'm Gergely" personal intro). The blog is a tab in the nav — not the primary surface.
- **Five-item nav with clear hierarchy.** `Newsletter | Podcast | Books | Blog | Investing` — each is a destination, not a category. "Investing" is unusual but signals the author's personal brand spans beyond just writing.
- **Strong personal anchor above the fold.** "Hi 👋, I'm Gergely" + author photo + book promo. Reader knows in 5 seconds who's writing and what they sell.

## What we could learn

- **Author-anchor on home page.** Signal Dispatch's home opens with the brand ("Signal Dispatch") + tagline ("Architecture, commerce, and the signals that matter"), but not with an author anchor. Adding a short "I'm Nino" + photo + 2-line frame above the post grid would serve the [[hiring-evaluator]] persona who needs to know whose voice this is.
- **A clear conversion path that isn't pushy.** Pragmatic Engineer's Subscribe CTA is one button, not three modal pops. Signal Dispatch could expose the RSS/newsletter affordance more visibly without violating the [[hiring-evaluator]] "no marketing tells" rule — one clean CTA in the footer or in-post.

## What to avoid

- **Don't lead with conversion.** Pragmatic Engineer is a paid Substack with a million-reader audience; the conversion-first home is calibrated to that scale. Signal Dispatch is a personal blog with a positioning function — leading with "Subscribe" reads as misaligned with the [[hiring-evaluator]] target.
- **Don't lean on social-proof numbers without the numbers.** "1 million readers" works because it's true; an unproven equivalent reads as posture. Skip until measurable.

## Implications for the prescription

- **[[F5]] nav rework sub-input**: 5-item nav with named destinations (not categories). Confirms [[lethain]]'s pattern from a different vantage.
- **Net-new affordance**: author anchor on home page — small "About Nino" block above the post grid. Lightweight version of Pragmatic Engineer's personal intro.
