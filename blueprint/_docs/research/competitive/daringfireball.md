## What they do well

- **Two-format typing on one chronological feed.** Posts come in two visible shapes: full Articles and Linked List items (marked with `★`). Both flow in reverse-chronological order on the home page, but the typography + the linked-source line + the `★` marker make the distinction legible without separating them onto different surfaces.
- **Author identity above the fold without performance.** "By John Gruber" sits at the top — clear attribution without resorting to a portrait or a bio block. The site identity *is* the author identity; no separate publication-brand performance.
- **RSS-first follow path.** No email capture; "Feeds / Social" in the primary nav. The publication has been running 20+ years on this posture without it killing growth — proof point for the v1-baseline-preserved no-email-capture rule.
- **Sponsorship as the only commercial affordance.** No paid tier, no membership, no "support me on Patreon." The sponsorship slot is acknowledged in nav and disposed of cleanly. Doesn't compromise the no-marketing-tells stance.

## What we could learn

- **Format-type markers on a unified feed.** v2's home page could surface format type (Post / Whitepaper / Counterpoint / Series-entry) via a marker similar to Gruber's `★` — keeps the chronological feed shape while making format legibility one glance instead of a click. Already in Stage 2 design principles as a card-level badge; Gruber confirms it works at the feed level too.
- **Long-running publication identity without re-branding.** Daring Fireball hasn't changed its visual identity meaningfully in over a decade. v2's brand register (D7) should be designed for the same kind of patience — picked once, expected to outlive several content-strategy pivots.
- **Sponsorship-as-clean commercial mode.** If v2 ever needs a revenue affordance, the Gruber model — single sponsor slot, clearly demarcated, no email-capture — fits the hiring-evaluator persona's no-marketing-tells rule. Don't borrow this unless that need surfaces; flag it as a reference if it ever does.

## What to avoid

- **Mixed-format feed with no filter affordance.** Gruber's home page is everything in chronological order with no filter chrome. Works for him because his cadence is daily and his audience is regular. v2's audience is mixed (peer architect, hiring evaluator, AI-curious IC) with very different read intents — a Library surface with format filtering (per Stage 3 plan) is the right adaptation. Don't import the "one feed, no filter" choice.
- **Daily-cadence-implied URL slugs.** Gruber's individual post URLs include the year + month + day. Works when you post daily. v2's posts are weeks apart — date-only URLs would surface gaps. v1's slug-only URLs are the right choice; preserve.

## Implications for v2

- **Format-type marker on the home feed** — a `Post` / `Whitepaper` / `Counterpoint` / `Series #N` badge per item, similar to Gruber's `★`. Already in [[02-design-principles.md]] at the card level; confirms it for the feed level too.
- **Author-identity-as-publication-identity is a viable v2 brand shape** — Thesis A pairs naturally with Gruber's posture (the publication is the author's body of work, brand is subordinate). For Thesis B (a more deliberate publication) Stratechery is the closer reference.
- **No revenue/membership affordance is a defensible long-run posture** — 20+ year run proves it. Confirms the no-email-capture decision is not a near-term constraint that gets relaxed when "scale needs it."
