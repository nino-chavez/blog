## Goal

Came for one of three things, in order of frequency (inferred from post composition, not measurement):

1. **A working pattern for agent-assisted production work** — chassis, contracts, validators, design-by-spec workflows. Not "prompt tips."
2. **A peer's read on a current industry argument** — "is the 10x productivity claim real?" "is process or inspection the right frame?" The counterpoint format exists for this.
3. **A reference architecture to cite** — long-form whitepapers as portable evidence in their own internal arguments.

## Read pattern

- Skim TL;DR / Executive Summary first; commit to body only if the framing earns it.
- Open whitepaper-format posts in a new tab; return later when they have time.
- Read on desktop more often than mobile (the work-context use case). The 1440 captures matter more here than the 375 captures.
- RSS-first or search-first entry; rarely browses tags or series cold.

## Drop reasons

- Long-form post with no within-page nav (F4 finding) — they bail rather than scroll-hunt to the section they came for.
- Hero-image-driven post-card grids (`home--1440.png`) without surfacing the substantive whitepapers/presentations except via right-rail. They're looking for the rigorous content, not the latest reflection.
- A post that claims a strong position without a Stage-2 receipt (the voice guide's "show your work" rule) — they pattern-match it as LinkedIn-thought-leadership and close the tab.

## What the redesign owes them

- **Within-post navigation on long-form** — TOC / anchor links / progress. This is the F4 prescription, scoped to this persona's habits.
- **Direct discovery of rigorous content** — promote whitepapers / counterpoints from "More" dropdown to primary nav, or restructure the home page so rigorous content is discoverable above the fold on desktop. This is F5.
- **A way to follow a thread without subscribing** — series-detail (F7 positive) already does this for one cohort of posts; the tag-detail pages (F1 failure) need the same affordance.
