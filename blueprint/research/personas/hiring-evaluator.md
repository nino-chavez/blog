## Goal

Showed up via:
- A LinkedIn post Nino published that links to a blog piece
- A portfolio link in an application
- A reference check ("here's what he's been writing")

Came to answer one question: **does the work back up the positioning?** "Architect who directs agents, not engineer who types code" is a strong claim — they're looking for receipts.

## Read pattern

- Reads top-to-bottom (no skim) for the first post — the post is the interview.
- Then samples 2-3 more posts to test for consistency. They want to know the voice isn't ghostwritten for a single piece.
- Almost always desktop (work context).
- May click through to a whitepaper to test rigor; rarely reads the whole whitepaper.
- High probability of also opening counterpoints to test for intellectual honesty.

## Drop reasons

- Inconsistent voice across posts — one rigorous piece followed by a LinkedIn-thought-leadership cliché tells them the rigorous one was a fluke.
- Marketing-y formatting — hero stock images, dense pull-quotes, "5 lessons learned" titles. The voice guide explicitly works against this; the IA shouldn't undercut it.
- Footer / about-page links that broadcast "personal brand" instead of "professional practice."

## What the redesign owes them

- **Cross-post coherence proof** — series pages (F7 positive) and counterpoint chains (F6 positive) already do this. Make sure the home page and post-detail surface them too, not just the dedicated index pages.
- **An About page or sidebar that frames "what this blog is" in the same voice as the posts** — currently the right-rail "Currently Surfacing" + "Recent Series" + "Also Here" does some of this work; whether it's enough is an F5 sub-question.
- **No marketing tells** — captures don't surface any obvious tells (no "Subscribe!" pop-ups, no email-gates, no "trending now" widgets). Maintain that posture through the redesign.
