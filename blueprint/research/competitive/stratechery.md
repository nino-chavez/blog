## What they do well

- **Author byline AS the brand position.** "By Ben Thompson" sits directly under the STRATECHERY wordmark — the publication name doesn't try to abstract away from the author. Establishes provenance in one beat.
- **Two-tier nav: About / Email/RSS / Linkedin/X + Explore / Companies / Topics.** First tier is "how to reach Ben"; second tier is "how to read the corpus." Each tier has a clear function.
- **Right-rail "Stratechery Plus" panel with member-only post titles.** Implicit social proof (these are the latest, this is the cadence) without overt marketing. Member CTA box is unobtrusive (small "Subscribe" + "Member Login").
- **Featured post with date and image.** "2026.21: The Data Center Veto" — uses issue-numbering convention that signals "this is a regular publication, not a sporadic blog." Counter-intuitive: the numbering creates trust.

## What we could learn

- **Issue-number or volume-number convention.** Signal Dispatch posts are dated but not numbered. A small `Dispatch #N` label on each post would frame the corpus as a deliberate publication, not a personal blog. Particularly serves [[hiring-evaluator]] who reads it as a portfolio object.
- **Two-tier nav distinguishing "how to reach the author" from "how to read the corpus."** Signal Dispatch's current nav mixes both (Blog / Series / Fiction / More + Tags / RSS / About in footer). Splitting them — content destinations in primary nav, reach-out / subscribe in secondary — would tighten both rails. Reinforces [[F5]] direction.
- **Right-rail recent-post panel with no images.** Title + date is enough when the surface is just "what's new" — no thumbnail needed. Signal Dispatch's right-rail "Currently Surfacing" + "Recent Series" do this but are denser; could trim.

## What to avoid

- **Paywall infrastructure.** Stratechery's Plus tier is the business model; Signal Dispatch has no equivalent and shouldn't pretend to. Skip the gating patterns entirely.
- **Issue-numbering can read as forced if the cadence is irregular.** Stratechery publishes 4×/week reliably. Signal Dispatch's weekly-ish cadence may not support the convention without a feeling of contrivance — worth piloting on a subset.

## Implications for the prescription

- **Author-anchor sub-input**: confirms [[pragmaticengineer]]'s pattern — author byline is part of the brand frame, not separate from it.
- **Two-tier nav consideration**: split content destinations from reach-out destinations in the primary nav. Companion question for [[F5]].
- **Pilot candidate**: Dispatch-numbering on new posts (don't retrofit). Test for 4-6 weeks before committing.
