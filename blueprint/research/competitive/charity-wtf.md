## What they do well

- **Minimal chrome.** Centered "CHARITY.WTF" title, hamburger menu top-right, single-column post body. No top nav, no sidebar, no widgets. The post IS the page.
- **Author byline + tags in left rail of post body.** `mipsytipsy` + tag list (`agentic development`, `agents`, `AI-SRE`, `context`, `data`, `observability 2.0`, `three pillars`) sit in the left margin of the post itself. Reader sees post metadata without scrolling.
- **WordPress-default typography in service of voice.** No custom styling that pulls attention from the words. Strong precedent that personality-first blogs don't need design polish to feel authoritative — the writing carries.

## What we could learn

- **Tags-in-margin pattern for post detail.** Signal Dispatch's `post-detail--1440.png` shows tag chips below the author byline horizontally. Moving them to a left-rail position would tighten the visual hierarchy and let the body breathe.
- **Single-column extreme minimalism is a valid choice.** Signal Dispatch shouldn't go this far (the cross-content-type relationship surfaces are an asset, not noise), but the underlying lesson — strip what doesn't earn its space — applies.

## What to avoid

- **No primary nav is a strong choice that limits discovery.** Charity's reader cohort knows who she is and arrives via direct/RSS/Twitter. Signal Dispatch needs to serve the [[ai-curious-ic]] and [[hiring-evaluator]] personas who arrive cold and need wayfinding. Hamburger-only nav would fail them.
- **WordPress aesthetic carries a "personal blog" connotation that misaligns with [[hiring-evaluator]] positioning.** Charity's voice is established enough to overcome it; Signal Dispatch's positioning ("architect who directs agents") is a stronger claim and needs the visual frame to support it.

## Implications for the prescription

- **Post-detail metadata layout**: consider moving tag chips to a left-rail position (small, secondary visual weight) rather than inline after the author byline. Net-positive for reading flow.
- **Reaffirms [[lethain]]'s minimal-chrome principle but with the constraint**: nav can shrink but cannot disappear, because the persona mix requires wayfinding.
