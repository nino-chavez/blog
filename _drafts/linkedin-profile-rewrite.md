# LinkedIn Profile — Headline + About Rewrite

**Status:** draft for review. Compared against current profile (screenshots 2026-04-19). Not a wholesale rewrite — the existing About is already 70% aligned with the reframe. Edits below sharpen two specific things: the headline, and the philosophy section of the About.

---

## Headline

**Current:**
> Product Architect at Commerce.com | AI-Native Commerce Platforms

**Proposed (primary):**
> Product Architect at Commerce.com | I direct agents, not engineers. Output ships.

**Proposed (softer alternative, if the sharper line feels too on-the-nose for first contact):**
> Product Architect at Commerce.com | AI-Native Platforms | Architect leverage, $0 team budget

**Proposed (most conservative — single phrase swap only):**
> Product Architect at Commerce.com | Shipping AI-native platforms at architect leverage

**Recommendation:** the primary version. LinkedIn headlines are one of the few surfaces where the clever + plain pairing is *expected* — a setup and a punchline. "I direct agents, not engineers" is the compressed claim; "Output ships" is the plain gloss that closes it. If a recruiter reads only the headline, that's the reframe in fourteen words.

## About — two targeted edits to the existing copy

Leave the opening four paragraphs as they are. The "25 years → strategy/execution collapsed → slides don't ship, prototypes do" arc is strong and I wouldn't touch it.

The philosophy section is where the edits go.

### Edit 1 — Philosophy item #1

**Current:**
> 1. Strategy is Code. I don't just design the system on a whiteboard; I help build it. I use AI agents to handle syntax so I can focus on architecture, logic, and intent.

**Proposed:**
> 1. Strategy is Code. I don't design on a whiteboard and hand it off. I hold the shape — stack choices, prompt architecture, security posture — and direct the execution layer. Agents type the code under direction. That part used to be ten engineers.

**Why:**
- "I help build it" is understating it in a way that invites the wrong question. The proposed version names what I actually do and what the old version of this job used to require.
- "That part used to be ten engineers" is the clever+plain move — it closes the category question before the reader can ask it.

### Edit 2 — add one line after Philosophy item #3

**After:**
> 3. Production is the Only Metric. Experiments are fun, but deployed URLs are what matter. I build systems that survive contact with real users - authentication, security, state - not just happy-path demos.

**Add:**
> The flex isn't what I built. It's that I shipped director-scope work with a team of zero.

**Why:** this is the new category-claim line. It belongs at the end of the philosophy section because it's the reframe the previous three points have been building toward. Short, blunt, and it uses "director-scope" and "team of zero" as the two anchors.

## About — one optional restructure

**Optional swap of paragraph four.**

**Current:**
> Now I combine decades of enterprise context with AI orchestration to ship production-grade systems in weeks, not months.

**Proposed:**
> Now I combine decades of enterprise context with AI orchestration to ship production-grade systems at architect leverage — strategy, architecture, and execution all collapsed into the same person, with agents as the team.

**Why:** "in weeks, not months" is a speed claim. Speed is table stakes now — every AI-native person on LinkedIn claims it. "Architect leverage with agents as the team" is a *shape* claim, and shape is the thing that's still rare.

Do this one only if the edits above feel conservative enough that you want a third.

## What NOT to change

- Don't retire the "Strategy and Execution collapsed" paragraph. It's the single best sentence in the current About and every profile visitor remembers it.
- Don't remove the "Recent builds" line. RallyHQ + AI Analyst Academy + commerce prototypes is concrete evidence; the reframe needs it to land.

- Don't drop "Product Architect at Commerce.com" from the headline. Title + employer is the trust anchor that makes the rest legible.
